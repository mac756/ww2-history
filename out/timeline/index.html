Timeline of World War II

From the rise of fascism in Italy to the atomic bombings of Japan — explore the key events that shaped the most devastating conflict in human history.

1922 - 1945

🏛1922

October 28, 1922

March on Rome

Mussolini's Blackshards march on Rome, demanding he be appointed Prime Minister. King Victor Emmanuel III invites Mussolini to form a government, marking the beginning of fascist rule in Italy.

💰1929

October 24, 1929

Black Thursday

The Wall Street Crash begins the Great Depression, triggering worldwide economic devastation. Unemployment soars, creating desperate conditions exploited by extremist movements.

🏛1933

January 30, 1933

Hitler Becomes Chancellor

Adolf Hitler is appointed Chancellor of Germany by President Paul von Hindenburg. Within months, he establishes a totalitarian Nazi dictatorship.

🏛1934

June 30, 1934

Night of Long Knives

Hitler purges the SA leadership and other potential rivals in a violent crackdown. Over 100 people are killed, securing Hitler's absolute control over the Nazi Party.

⚔1935

October 3, 1935

Italian Invasion of Ethiopia

Italy invades Ethiopia from Italian Somaliland and Eritrea. Despite Ethiopian resistance, the League of Nations fails to stop the aggression, demonstrating its weakness.

⚔1936

July 17, 1936

Spanish Civil War Begins

Military uprising in Spain triggers the Spanish Civil War. Nazi Germany and Fascist Italy support Franco's Nationalists, while the USSR supports the Republic. This conflict serves as a proxy war for WWII.

🏛1936

August 1, 1936

Berlin Olympics

Nazi Germany hosts the Summer Olympics in Berlin. Jesse Owens wins four gold medals, contradicting Nazi theories of racial supremacy.

🏛1936

October 25, 1936

Rome-Berlin Axis

Italy and Germany form the Rome-Berlin Axis, a formal alliance marking the beginning of what will become the Axis Powers.

⚔1937

April 26, 1937

Bombing of Guernica

German and Italian air forces bomb the Spanish town of Guernica, destroying 70% of the town and killing hundreds. It becomes a symbol of civilian targeting in modern warfare.

🏛1938

March 12, 1938

Anschluss of Austria

Germany annexes Austria (Anschluss) in violation of the Treaty of Versailles. Austrian Nazis install Arthur Seyss-Inquart as Chancellor, and German troops enter Austria.

🏛1938

September 30, 1938

Munich Agreement

Britain and France appeas Hitler by signing the Munich Agreement, allowing Germany to annex the Sudetenland in Czechoslovakia. Chamberlain declares 'peace in our time.'

🏛1939

November 9-10, 1939

Kristallnacht

Nazi persecution of Jews escalates in the 'Night of Broken Glass.' Synagogues are burned, Jewish businesses destroyed, and thousands arrested. This marks a turning point in Nazi persecution.

🏛1939

August 23, 1939

Molotov-Ribbentrop Pact

Germany and Soviet Union sign the non-aggression pact, secretly dividing Eastern Europe into spheres of influence. This clears the way for the invasion of Poland.

⚔1939

September 1, 1939

Invasion of Poland

Germany invades Poland, beginning World War II. Using blitzkrieg tactics, German forces overwhelm Polish defenses. Britain and France declare war on September 3.

⚔1940

November 30, 1939

Winter War Begins

Soviet Union invades Finland, beginning the Winter War. Despite Finnish resistance, the USSR's superior numbers eventually force Finland to sue for peace in March 1940.

⚔1940

April 9, 1940

Operation Weserübung

Germany invades Denmark and Norway using blitzkrieg tactics. Denmark falls in hours, while Norway holds out until June despite Allied assistance.

⚔1940

May 10, 1940

Invasion of France & Low Countries

Germany launches blitzkrieg invasion of France and the Low Countries. Within weeks, Netherlands, Belgium, and Luxembourg fall. French forces retreat to Dunkirk.

⚔1940

May 26-June 4, 1940

Operation Dynamo (Dunkirk)

Allied forces are evacuated from Dunkirk, France. Over 338,000 soldiers are rescued across the English Channel in one of the largest evacuations in history.

⚔1940

July 10, 1940

Battle of Britain

The Luftwaffe begins bombing Britain in preparation for invasion. RAF pilots heroically defend British skies in the first major military defeat of Hitler's forces.

🏛1940

July 10, 1940

Vichy France Established

After France's surrender, Marshal Pétain establishes Vichy France as a Nazi puppet state in southern France. General de Gaulle escapes to London to lead the Free French.

⚔1941

June 22, 1941

Operation Barbarossa

Germany invades the Soviet Union in the largest military operation in history. Three million German troops cross the border, beginning the Eastern Front.

⚔1941

December 7, 1941

Attack on Pearl Harbor

Japan launches a surprise attack on the US Pacific Fleet at Pearl Harbor, Hawaii. Over 2,400 Americans are killed. The US declares war on Japan the next day, entering WWII.

⚔1942

June 4-7, 1942

Battle of Midway

US Navy defeats Japanese carrier fleet at Midway, turning the tide in the Pacific War. Four Japanese aircraft carriers are destroyed, limiting Japan's offensive capability.

⚔1942

October 23, 1942

Battle of El Alamein

British 8th Army under Montgomery defeats Axis forces at El Alamein, Egypt. This marks the turning point in North Africa, ending Axis hopes of controlling the Mediterranean.

⚔1942

November 8, 1942

Operation Torch

Allied forces land in French North Africa (Morocco, Algeria, Tunisia). Vichy French forces initially resist but soon join the Allies under General Eisenhower.

⚔1943

February 2, 1943

Battle of Stalingrad Ends

German 6th Army surrenders at Stalingrad, ending one of the bloodiest battles in history. Over 850,000 Axis casualties. This marks the turning point on the Eastern Front.

⚔1943

July 9, 1943

Operation Husky (Sicily)

Allied forces launch the invasion of Sicily, the first major amphibious operation in the Mediterranean. The island is secured by August 17, leading to Mussolini's downfall.

⚔1943

July 5-August 23, 1943

Battle of Kursk

Largest tank battle in history. Soviet forces repel the German offensive at Kursk, destroying the last major German offensive capability on the Eastern Front.

⚔1944

June 6, 1944

Operation Overlord (D-Day)

Allied forces launch the largest amphibious invasion in history on the beaches of Normandy, France. 156,000 troops land, beginning the liberation of Western Europe.

⚔1944

September 17, 1944

Operation Market Garden

Allied airborne operation to capture bridges in the Netherlands fails. The 'bridge too far' becomes a costly defeat, delaying the Allied advance into Germany.

⚔1944

December 16, 1944

Battle of the Bulge

Germany launches a surprise offensive in the Ardennes, creating a 'bulge' in Allied lines. After severe fighting, Allies repel the attack by January 1945.

🏛1945

February 4-11, 1945

Yalta Conference

Roosevelt, Churchill, and Stalin meet at Yalta to discuss post-war Europe. Agreement on Germany's division, UN formation, and Soviet entry into war against Japan.

⚔1945

February 19, 1945

Battle of Iwo Jima

US Marines capture the island of Iwo Jima after 36 days of brutal fighting. The iconic flag-raising on Mount Suribachi becomes a symbol of American resolve.

🏛1945

May 8, 1945

V-E Day

Germany surrenders unconditionally. Victory in Europe is declared. Eisenhower accepts the surrender in Reims. The war in Europe ends after nearly six years.

⚔1945

April 1-June 21, 1945

Battle of Okinawa

US forces capture Okinawa after 82 days of fighting. The last major battle of the Pacific War results in over 200,000 casualties and convinces US planners to use atomic bombs.

🏛1945

July 17-August 2, 1945

Potsdam Conference

Truman, Churchill (later Attlee), and Stalin meet to discuss post-war Europe. The conference addresses Germany's future, war crimes trials, and the Soviet Union's role in Asia.

⚔1945

August 6 & 9, 1945

Atomic Bombings

US drops atomic bombs on Hiroshima (August 6) and Nagasaki (August 9). Japan surrenders on August 15, becoming the only nation to suffer nuclear attack.

🏛1945

September 2, 1945

V-J Day

Japan formally surrenders aboard the USS Missouri in Tokyo Bay. General MacArthur accepts the surrender. World War II officially ends.
